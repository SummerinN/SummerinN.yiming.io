Finding Humanism from the Eastern Most

This is my essay report for my reserach in the Unit 1 of my Master study, which is currently being reviewed.


Yiming Jiang, Student Number: 23010335
Ma Design for Data Visualization, London College of Communication, University of the Arts, London.


This article reviews Takeo Nakano's design projects, analyzes his methods, and explores the cultural background that shaped his data visualization approach: the Japanese cultural background. In addition, my goal is to reveal the commonalities and differences in their design philosophies, among which Giorgia Lupi's data humanism is the main idea, Tufte's modernist design ideas, and Catherine D'Ignazio and Lauren F. Klein's data feminism is the main idea. Finally, based on the above comparison, this article summarizes some data visualization techniques that designers can refer to.
